How Carbon Credits Work

By using your clean cooking stove instead of traditional cooking methods, you're reducing carbon emissions that harm our environment.

These reductions are measured as carbon credits, which have real financial value in global markets. We share this value with you through rewards.

About Carbon Credits

What Are Carbon Credits?

Carbon credits represent the reduction of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. One carbon credit equals one tonne of carbon dioxide (CO₂) or equivalent gases not emitted.

When you use your clean cooking stove instead of traditional methods like charcoal or wood, you prevent carbon emissions that would have been released.

How We Calculate Your Impact

Your stove tracks actual usage data, which we use to calculate how much carbon you've saved compared to traditional cooking methods.

1 hour of cooking with charcoal ≈ 1.2 kg CO₂
1 hour of cooking with your stove ≈ 0.2 kg CO₂
Carbon saved per hour ≈ 1.0 kg CO₂

How You Benefit

The carbon credits generated by our users are verified by international standards and sold on global markets. We share this value with you through:

  • Direct rewards in cooking credit
  • Mobile money transfers
  • Discounts on accessories and upgrades
  • Community improvement projects

Environmental Impact

Beyond the financial benefits, your choice to use clean cooking technology helps:

  • Reduce deforestation and habitat loss
  • Improve local air quality and health
  • Combat global climate change
  • Preserve resources for future generations
